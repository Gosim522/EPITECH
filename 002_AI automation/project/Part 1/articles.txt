Title: Goodbye Soggy Fries: Cafeteria Unveils Bold New Menu

The campus cafeteria reopened Monday with a redesigned menu, and the
lunch line tells the story. Out went the tired sandwiches; in came

fresher, healthier options. Prices stayed the same, a detail few
students missed. Whether the buzz lasts beyond opening week remains to
be seen — but for now, the cafeteria is the busiest spot on campus.

--- ARTICLE 2 — All-night coding session ---

[EN]
Title: Caffeine and Code: Inside Campus's All-Night Hackathon

The third-floor lab never went dark this weekend. More than sixty
students powered through a 24-hour coding marathon, fueled by pizza,
energy drinks, and sheer stubbornness. Teams raced to build working
apps before the noon deadline, from a campus carpool tracker to a
study-buddy matcher. "Around 4 a.m. everything either breaks or finally
works," laughed organizer Devon Park. Mentors roamed the room offering
debugging help and quiet encouragement. By morning, bleary eyes met
working demos. The winning team earned bragging rights — and, perhaps
more importantly, a full day of sleep.

--- ARTICLE 3 — Student gaming tournament ---

[EN]
Title: Game On: Student Tournament Draws Record Crowd

The auditorium roared Friday night as two hundred students packed in
for the semester's biggest esports tournament. Sixteen teams battled
across three games, with the final decided by a single, nail-biting
round. "I've never heard the room that loud," said event host Aisha
Cole. Beyond the competition, the night doubled as a fundraiser for the
student tech fund. Organizers say interest doubled from last year,
hinting at a permanent league next semester. For winners and spectators
alike, the message was clear: campus gaming is no longer a niche — it's
an event.

With finals just two weeks away, the library is filling fast and the

just memory.
